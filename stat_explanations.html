Overall Test Complexity

Description of the Metric

Overall Test Complexity (OTC) quantifies the global difficulty of a test across its entire measurement range.

It is conceptually related to metrics such as Hardness and Sample-Independent Hardness, but extends beyond these by capturing the average cognitive ability demand imposed by the full test structure.

OTC is independent of the sample, as it is computed exclusively from the normalized scores.

OTC is expressed as a continuous value in the open interval (0,1).

Definition

\( R_{\max} \) denote the maximum raw score of the test.

\( SS(r) \) denote the standard score or IQ score (mean = 100, standard deviation = 15) associated with raw score \( r \) according to the test norms.

\( M_{\mathrm{ap}} = 138.5 \) is the reference ability level (median ability of the high-range population).

\( \sigma = 15 \) is the standard deviation.

\[ A = \frac{1}{R_{\max}} \sum_{r=1}^{R_{\max}} SS(r) \]
\[ C = \frac{A - M_{\mathrm{ap}}}{\sigma} \]
\[ \mathrm{OTC} = \frac{1}{1 + \exp(-C)} \]
